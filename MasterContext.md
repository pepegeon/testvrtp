# Master Context: XR User Evaluation Study on Immersion

## 1. Nature and Purpose of This Study

This is a **student-led XR user evaluation study** whose primary goal is to **investigate immersion as a multi-parameter, interdependent phenomenon** across different XR platforms (e.g., VR headsets and mobile XR).

The study is **not** about:
- maximizing realism
- benchmarking hardware performance
- evaluating ethical or social impacts

Instead, it treats **immersion as a design and experiential construct**, shaped jointly by system parameters and user experience.

The study adopts **established XR questionnaires** (IPQ, SSQ, GEQ) as baseline instruments, supplemented by **qualitative observation and probing**, to explain *why* immersion changes — not just *whether* it does.

---

## 2. Definition of Immersion (Shared Understanding)

### 2.1 What Immersion Is

In this study, **immersion is understood as the degree to which a user’s perceptual, cognitive, and motor resources are allocated to the mediated environment rather than the physical world**.

Immersion emerges from:
- system properties (what the XR system affords and constrains)
- user responses (attention, engagement, comfort, agency)

Immersion is **measurable indirectly** through constructs such as:
- presence
- flow
- engagement
- cybersickness (as a limiting factor)

---

### 2.2 What Immersion Is *Not*

**Immersion ≠ Realism**

- A system can feel immersive without being visually realistic (e.g., stylized games).
- A highly realistic system can fail to be immersive if interaction, motion, or feedback break engagement.

Realism is treated as **one possible contributor**, not the goal or definition of immersion.

---

## 3. Immersion and the Reality–Virtuality Continuum

This study situates XR experiences along **Milgram & Kishino’s Reality–Virtuality Continuum**, from:
- physical reality
- augmented reality
- mixed reality
- fully virtual environments

However, **movement along the continuum does not guarantee increased immersion**.

Key position of this study:
> Immersion does not monotonically increase as experiences move toward full virtuality; instead, immersion depends on how system parameters align with human perception, action, and cognition.

This means:
- mobile XR may outperform VR on comfort or usability
- VR may outperform mobile XR on spatial presence
- immersion may fragment across dimensions rather than increase uniformly

---

## 4. Immersion as a Multi-Parameter System

Immersion is treated as a **result of interacting parameters**, not isolated features.

### Core System Parameters Studied
- **Rendering fidelity** (visual stability, clarity, motion coherence)
- **Interaction design** (input affordances, responsiveness, agency)
- **Locomotion design** (movement methods, sensory conflict, control)

### Core User Experience Outcomes
- **Presence** (sense of “being there”)
- **Flow** (absorption and challenge–skill balance)
- **Cybersickness** (discomfort that limits immersion)

These parameters are **interdependent**:
- Improvements in one dimension may amplify or suppress others
- Some combinations produce emergent effects not predicted by single parameters

---

## 5. Discovery-Oriented Framing of Immersion

The study explicitly allows for:
- partial immersion
- conflicting immersion signals
- emergence of **new experiential dimensions** not fully captured by existing questionnaires

Standardized surveys are used as **anchors**, not as the final truth.

If a pattern appears that is:
- repeatedly observed
- not well explained by presence, flow, or sickness alone

…it is treated as a **candidate new dimension or nuance of immersion**, to be described qualitatively and analytically.

---

## 6. Structure of the Research Team

The team is divided into **functional subteams**, but all subteams contribute to understanding immersion holistically.

### 6.1 Experimental Subteams (Applied Focus)

Each experimental subteam:
- focuses on one *primary system parameter*
- administers **the same core questionnaires**
- supplements with **parameter-specific observations and probes**
- interprets results **in relation to all other parameters**, not in isolation

#### Subteams:
- **Rendering Subteam**
- **Interaction Subteam**
- **Locomotion Subteam**

Important constraint:
> No subteam studies “their parameter alone.”  
> All analyses must consider how parameters interact to shape immersion.

---

### 6.2 Theoretical Specialist Subteams (Analytical Focus)

Each theoretical specialist:
- conducts literature research on **how their assigned parameter contributes to immersion**
- identifies:
  - known mechanisms
  - trade-offs
  - contradictions or limitations in prior research
- supports interpretation of empirical findings

Their role is **not** to validate the experiment, but to:
> Explain *why* observed immersion patterns make sense (or don’t) in light of XR theory.

---

## 7. Methodological Alignment

### Quantitative Anchors
- **IPQ** → Presence
- **SSQ** → Cybersickness
- **GEQ (short)** → Flow / Engagement

These provide:
- comparability
- academic legitimacy
- baseline interpretation

### Qualitative Augmentation
- behavioral observation
- user hesitation, adaptation, avoidance
- short reflective probes

These provide:
- explanatory depth
- insight into interdependencies
- discovery of unmeasured dimensions

---

## 8. Shared Research Philosophy

This study treats XR immersion as:
- a **design problem**
- a **systems problem**
- a **user experience phenomenon**

The goal is not to prove that one platform or parameter is “better,” but to:
> Understand how different configurations of XR systems produce different immersive outcomes — and why.

All team members and their AI assistants should operate within this shared conceptual framework.

---

## 9. Instruction to AI Assistants Using This Context

When assisting with this project, the AI should:
- respect the distinction between immersion and realism
- treat immersion as multi-dimensional and interdependent
- prioritize interpretation over raw measurement
- support both empirical and theoretical contributions
- avoid siloed reasoning tied to a single parameter

The AI’s role is to help clarify, analyze, and synthesize — not to collapse immersion into a single score or definition.
