# Locomotion Subteam: Research & Mapping Documentation

## 1. Overview
This document outlines the relationship between locomotion design and the study's core parameters: immersion, presence, flow, and cybersickness. In accordance with the study's philosophy, locomotion is treated as a multi-parameter system where movement methods must align with human perception and motor resources.

## 3. Expected Outcomes (Theoretical Hypotheses)
These hypotheses provide a baseline for the "Expected vs. Observed" comparison notes.

### 3.1 Cybersickness Likelihood (SSQ)
* **Physical Movement**: Lowest expected sickness; visual and vestibular cues are perfectly aligned.
* **Teleportation**: Low nausea risk; however, it may trigger "disorientation" (SSQ-D) due to sudden position changes.
* **Smooth Locomotion**: Highest expected sickness; sustained conflict between perceived visual motion and physical stillness.

### 3.2 Presence Stability (IPQ)
* **Physical Movement**: Expected to provide the most stable "Place Illusion" by utilizing natural motor resources.
* **Smooth Locomotion**: High spatial presence potential, but "brittle"; sickness symptoms will cause presence to collapse immediately.
* **Teleportation**: Fragmented presence; users may feel they are observing a world rather than inhabiting it during transitions.

### 3.3 Flow Support (GEQ)
* **Smooth Locomotion**: Supports Flow by allowing continuous movement that matches the user's navigational skill.
* **Teleportation**: Potential Flow disruption; the "point-and-blink" mechanic acts as a constant interface reminder.
* **Hybrid (e.g., Arm-Swinging)**: May support Flow via increased engagement, but risks disruption if the mechanic is too physically demanding.
