# XR Immersion Parameters — Short-Form Definitions

Immersion in XR emerges from the interaction between **system properties** (what the technology provides) and **user experience properties** (how the user perceives and responds).  
Neither operates independently; immersion is the result of their coupling.

---

## 1. System Properties (Objective / Designable)

These parameters are controlled by hardware, software, rendering, and interaction design.

### Display & Visual Fidelity
**The quality and stability of visual output.**  
Includes field of view (FOV), 
resolution, 
refresh rate, 
stereoscopy, and 
motion-to-photon latency.

    Directly influences visual immersion and comfort.


### Rendering Stability
**The consistency of visual rendering over time.**  
Includes frame rate stability, 
motion smoothness, 
lighting coherence, and 
distortion control.  

    Critical for preventing cybersickness.

### Tracking Fidelity
**How accurately user movement is captured and mapped.**  
Includes head tracking, 
hand/controller tracking, and 
positional drift.  

    Supports spatial consistency and presence.

### Degrees of Freedom (DoF)
**The range of movement supported by the system.**  
3DoF supports rotation only; 
6DoF supports full spatial movement.  

    Higher DoF enables embodied interaction.

### Interaction Fidelity
**The naturalness and precision of user input.**  
Includes controllers, 
hand tracking, 
gestures, and 
haptics.  

    Strongly affects agency and flow.

### Locomotion Design
**How users move through virtual space.**  
Includes teleportation, 
smooth movement, 
snap turning, or 
physical walking. 

    A major contributor to immersion–comfort trade-offs. 
    //trade off could be the "realism" part?

### Multisensory Feedback
**Non-visual sensory cues that reinforce interaction.**  
Includes spatial audio 
and haptics. 

    Enhances coherence and realism.

## 2. User Experience Properties (Subjective / Emergent)

These parameters describe how users psychologically and physically experience XR.

### Immersion (User-Level)
**The subjective feeling of being surrounded by and engaged in the virtual environment.**  
Emerges from coherent and compelling system properties.

### Presence
**The sensation of ‘being there’ in the virtual space.**  
Includes spatial presence, 
self-presence, and 
social presence.  

    Highly sensitive to tracking accuracy and latency.

### Flow
**A state of deep engagement where action and awareness merge.**  
Occurs when challenges, controls, and feedback are well balanced.

**The feeling that one’s actions have direct and meaningful effects.**

### Comfort
**The user’s physical tolerance of the experience.**  
A prerequisite for sustained immersion and flow.

### Cybersickness
**A form of motion sickness induced by sensory mismatch in XR.**  
Includes nausea, 
dizziness, 
disorientation, and 
eye strain.  

    Often caused by latency, visual instability, or artificial locomotion.

---

## 3. Key Interdependencies (Design Insight)

- Increased visual immersion | can increase presence | but also raise cybersickness risk.
- Higher interaction fidelity | can enhance agency | but increase cognitive load.
- Locomotion methods mediate the trade-off between | spatial presence | and | comfort |.
- Cybersickness disrupts | presence | and | collapses flow |.

Immersion is therefore not maximized by any single parameter, but by balancing interdependent system and user properties.
