# How to Go About Qualitative Questions (Mindset)

Quantitative surveys (IPQ, CSQ, GEQ) tell you what changed.  
Qualitative questions tell you **why** it changed and **where** it came from.

---

## [[initialial questions generated]]

### 1) “What moment felt the most immersive to you?”
(You may describe a specific scene, action, or interaction.)

📌 **What this captures:**
- Peak immersion
- Positive system contributions
- Flow alignment

📎 **Taggable to:**
- Rendering fidelity
- Interaction
- Environmental design

---

### 2) “Did anything break your immersion or pull you out of the experience?”
(You may describe a specific scene, action, or interaction.)

📌 **What this captures:**
- Immersion loss / breakdown points
- Negative triggers (what caused the drop)
- Where and when the experience “fell apart”

📎 **Taggable to:**
- Rendering fidelity
- Interaction
- Environmental design

---

### 3) “How did movement or interaction affect your comfort or engagement?”
(For example: walking, turning, grabbing, using tools.)

📌 **What this captures:**
- Comfort vs engagement trade-offs
- Embodiment issues
- Cybersickness-related cues

📎 **Taggable to:**
- Locomotion methods
- Interaction design
- Control mapping

---

## Why This Set Works (You Can Use This in the Report)

This trio:
- Covers immersion gain
- Covers immersion loss
- Covers mechanism (movement / interaction)

Together, they form a closed diagnostic loop:  
**Where immersion happened → where it failed → why it changed.**

---

## [[added questions (subteam probes)]]

**How to use these:**  
Ask the 3 core questions above for everyone.  
Then only ask the relevant subteam probes based on what the participant mentions (don’t ask all).

---

### Rendering subteam (follow-up probes)

1. Can you describe a moment when the visuals broke your immersion? What did you notice, and what were you doing at the time?
2. Can you describe a moment when the visuals felt most real or convincing? What do you think made it feel that way?
3. Did you feel that the world/object positions looked “off”? If yes, when did it happen and what were you doing at the time?
4. Did you notice any lag, stutter, or jitter? If yes, when did it happen and how did it affect your experience?
5. Did the visuals ever make it harder to play or interact (e.g., aiming, selecting, reading)? When?
6. Did the visuals ever feel inconsistent (e.g., quality changing, objects popping in/out, sudden blur)? If yes, when?
7. How was the readability of important things (UI text, icons, targets)? When was it easiest or hardest to see?
8. Did lighting or shadows affect immersion (helped or distracted)? Can you describe a moment?
9. Did the visuals match your expectations of scale and distance (how big/far things felt)? If not, when did it feel wrong?

---

### Locomotion subteam (follow-up probes)

1. Have you ever felt motion sickness when you were playing? What were you doing in the game to make you feel that way?
2. What kind of movement actions felt most comfortable for you? Why?
3. What kind of movement actions felt least comfortable for you? Why?
4. Did you feel in control of movement the whole time? If not, when did you feel you “lost control” or felt surprised by movement?
5. Did movement help you feel more immersed in the world, or did it pull you out instead? Can you describe a moment that shows this?
6. How easy was it to navigate to where you wanted to go? What parts felt easy or hard?
7. Did turning feel okay (smooth turning / snap turning / rotating the phone)? When did turning feel best or worst?
8. Did movement speed feel right? If not, when did it feel too fast or too slow?
9. Did movement ever make you lose your sense of direction (get disoriented)? When did that happen?

---

### User-interaction subteam (follow-up probes)

1. Was it clear what you could interact with? If not, what was confusing?
2. Did the controls/actions feel natural or awkward? Why?
3. Can you describe a moment when interacting felt smooth and satisfying? What made it feel smooth?
4. Can you describe a moment when interacting felt frustrating or slow? What caused it?
5. Did you feel like the system responded clearly to your actions (feedback)? If not, when did you feel unsure if something worked?
6. How easy was it to learn the interactions at the start? What helped you learn, or what was missing?
7. Did you ever hesitate because you weren’t sure what would happen when you interacted? When?
8. Did you make mistakes while interacting (misclicks, wrong actions)? What caused them?
9. How easy was it to recover from mistakes (undo, cancel, try again)? Can you give an example?

---

## Optional: Skip Logic (to keep interviews short)

- Start with the 3 core questions.
- If the participant mentions **visuals/performance/readability** → ask 1–3 rendering probes.
- If they mention **comfort/turning/navigation/sickness** → ask 1–3 locomotion probes.
- If they mention **controls/confusion/feedback/mistakes** → ask 1–3 interaction probes.
