How to Go About Qualitative Questions (Mindset)

Quantitative surveys (IPQ, CSQ, GEQ) tell you what changed.
Qualitative questions tell you why it changed and where it came from.

[[initialial questions generated]]
“What moment felt the most immersive to you?”
(You may describe a specific scene, action, or interaction.)
    📌 What this captures:
    Peak immersion
    Positive system contributions
    Flow alignment

    📎 Taggable to:
    Rendering fidelity
    Interaction
    Environmental design

“Did anything break your immersion or pull you out of the experience?”
(You may describe a specific scene, action, or interaction.)
    📌 What this captures:
    Peak immersion
    Positive system contributions
    Flow alignment

    📎 Taggable to:
    Rendering fidelity
    Interaction
    Environmental design


“How did movement or interaction affect your comfort or engagement?”
(For example: walking, turning, grabbing, using tools.)
    📌 What this captures:
    Comfort vs engagement trade-offs
    Embodiment issues
    Cybersickness-related cues

    📎 Taggable to:
    Locomotion methods
    Interaction design
    Control mapping

Why This Set Works (You Can Use This in the Report)

This trio:

Covers immersion gain

Covers immersion loss

Covers mechanism (movement / interaction)

Together, they form a closed diagnostic loop:

Where immersion happened → where it failed → why it changed.
