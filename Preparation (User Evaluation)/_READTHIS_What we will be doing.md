# XR User Evaluation Study — Subteam Roles & Evaluation Plan

## 🎯 Study Purpose
**Research Question (refined):**  
> *How do interdependent XR system parameters — specifically rendering, interaction, and locomotion — jointly influence immersion, presence, flow, and cybersickness across VR and mobile XR platforms?*

This study uses **established questionnaires** and **supplementary observation/probing** to explain *why* these scores vary across platforms and parameter conditions.

---

## 🧠 Subteam Responsibilities

### 1️⃣ Rendering Fidelity Subteam
**Focus:** Visual parameters (frame stability, motion consistency, optical quality)

#### Established Tools
- **Igroup Presence Questionnaire (IPQ)** — measure presence (spatial, involvement, realism) :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}
- **Simulator Sickness Questionnaire (SSQ)** — measure cybersickness severity :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

#### Interdependent Parameters
- *Rendering stability → Presence*  
- *Visual quality → Cybersickness potential*

#### Tasks
- Administer IPQ & SSQ after each session
- Log visual quality settings per session
- Note frame drops, visual artefacts during play
- Supplement with probes like “did any visual glitch distract you?”

#### How to Conduct
- Use paper or digital forms immediately post-session  
- Ensure consistent lighting + headset calibration  
- Avoid interrupting immersive experience (administer outside XR) :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

👉 Best resources: IPQ instrument definitions and guidance such as in Schwind et al. presence studies :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

---

### 2️⃣ Locomotion Methods Subteam
**Focus:** How users move and navigate (teleport, smooth movement, physical steps)

#### Established Tools
- **SSQ** — capture discomfort linked to motion conflict :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
- **Flow measures** (e.g., Flow Short Scale or selected items from GEQ) — assess engagement level

#### Interdependent Parameters
- *Locomotion → Spatial Presence & Comfort*  
- *Movement design → Cybersickness vs Flow trade-off*

#### Tasks
- Separate participants into locomotion conditions (e.g., teleport vs smooth)  
- Collect SSQ scores after each locomotion condition  
- Use a short flow survey after each session

#### How to Conduct
- Standardize session duration (e.g., 5–7 minutes per test)  
- Observe hesitation, avoidance, repeated navigational errors  
- Ask probes like “what part of moving around felt uncomfortable?”

👉 Locomotion design is a key cause of sensory conflict and sickness; observation complements SSQ results :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

---

### 3️⃣ Interaction Subteam
**Focus:** Input methods (controllers, touch, gesture) and user agency

#### Established Tools
- **Presence/immersion scales** (IPQ or short presence items from mixed UX tools) :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
- **Flow/Gaming Engagement Questionnaires** (selected GEQ/flow items)

#### Interdependent Parameters
- *Affordances → Agency → Flow*  
- *Interaction complexity → Cognitive load → Immersion interruptions*

#### Tasks
- Record task success rates (e.g., number of correct interactions)
- Track interaction errors, repeated attempts
- Administer post-session flow questions

#### How to Conduct
- Keep a **task checklist** (e.g., pick up object, activate target)  
- Log retries, time-to-success per task  
- Ask contextual probes like “did the controls feel intuitive?”

👉 Use established engagement/flow items from literature to ground subjective measures :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

## 📊 Interdependency Mapping

The following shows *how the questionnaires tie to immersive parameter interactions*:

| Parameter | Questionnaire | Interdependency Insight |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------------|
| Rendering Stability | IPQ, SSQ | High rendering fidelity increases presence but can raise sickness if unstable |
| Locomotion | SSQ, Flow | Continuous motion affects spatial presence and comfort (sickness ↔ flow tension) |
| Interaction Fidelity | Flow, Presence | Better affordances enhance flow and sense of agency, but complex input can reduce flow |

## 🧪 Conducting the Tests — Good Practices

### 📍 1. Before Sessions
- Ensure **baseline comfort** (e.g., rest 5 min before entering XR)  
- Brief participants on how to fill questionnaires  
- Standardize environment (lighting, seating, audio)

### 📍 2. During Sessions
- **Do not interrupt presence** — administer questionnaires *after* test sessions :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}  
- Observe visible discomfort (remove headset, posture changes)

### 📍 3. After Sessions
- Collect surveys in the same order each time (e.g., SSQ → IPQ → Flow)  
- Use Likert scale items consistently (e.g., 7-point for IPQ items) :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

### 📍 4. Supplement With Probes
Use short, focused questions such as:
- “What moment felt the most real or distracting?”
- “Did you feel movement mismatch at any point?”
- “Did controls ever interrupt your focus?”

These contextual responses **explain quantitative scores**.

## 🧠 Notes on Analysis

### Quantitative
- Calculate subscale means (e.g., IPQ spatial presence, SSQ total score)
- Compare across platforms (e.g., VR vs mobile) and locomotion conditions

### Qualitative
- Thematic coding of probe responses
- Behavioral indicators (hesitation, task retries)

---

## 🧩 Final Tips
- Standard questionnaires provide **baseline, defensible measurement**  
- Observations + targeted probes *explain why* scores differ  
- Interdependencies (e.g., rendering ↔ locomotion ↔ interaction) should be **highlighted in your analysis, not just reported**
